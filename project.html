Blockchain Development

Public.
A public blockchain is a distributed virtual ledger that operates on a peer-to-peer network and can be seen, read, or written to by anyone. Because no permission is necessary to access or participate in it, it is also known as a permissionless blockchain. Because of the decentralized, distributed character, public blockchains are governed by all of their members rather than a single center of authority or decision-making.

Private.
A private blockchain is a centralized P2P (peer-to-peer) network that connects all nodes to a single point that oversees the whole platform. Private blockchains are mostly chosen by banks and businesses to facilitate seamless operations. When a member attempts to engage in a private blockchain, the network's center point requests permission. Private blockchains are substantially quicker than public blockchains.

Hybrid blockchain.
Depending on their architecture, hybrid blockchains fall somewhere between private and public blockchains. In a hybrid blockchain, transactions and data are rendered private but may be validated as necessary by granting access through a smart contract. Private information is stored inside the network but may still be verified. Users of a hybrid blockchain get full network access. Unless they participate in a transaction, the user's identity is safeguarded and shielded from other users. The opposite party is then informed of their identification.

Sidechain.
A sidechain is basically a separate network that is linked to the parent blockchain or mainchain but operates independently. The link between the two is represented by a two-way peg. The creation of sidechains has immensely helped legacy blockchain protocols such as Bitcoin and Ethereum.
